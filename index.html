     The Text Mining Group at National University of Defense Technology is conducted in the Computer Science Department and Research Center of System Software.The group consists of 2 faculty who serve as principal investigators on multiple projects, and who advise graduate students at all levels. We focuse on extracting knowledge from biomedical texts (including biomedical papers, clinical records, FDA reports, and so on),building knowledge graph and helping medical staff make better decision through inference.Our research benefits from close collaboration with Xiangya School of Medicine, Central South University in related fields such as drug-drug interaction and targeted drug therapy.To learn more about our group, please visit our Labs, Groups and Projects page.We are also researching on Group Profile and User Profile for instant-messaging tools, such as QQ and Wechat, collaboration with some Internet Companies.

Recent Work

     The system is based on Hadoop ecology maintenance, adding HBase and Hive to manage structured or unstructured data to facilitate the storage of SQL, NoSQL data. Existing text, audio, video, pictures, links, applets and other data resources. In the near future, we hope to complete the tagging of WeChat groups through the data in the current and near-term plans. Focusing on the goal of group tagging, the following subdivided goals have been subdivided in the previous period:
     1. High frequency word classification statistics.
     High-frequency words are extracted from the group's internal chat messages, group information, etc., and high-frequency words are categorized into statistics to form the distribution of high-frequency words.
     2. Group user relationship chain analysis.
    The group membership information and intra-group user interaction relationship are analyzed and classified to form a hierarchy of user relationship chains.
     3. Analyze the core circle in the group.
     Through intra-group chat messages, statistical analysis was conducted to find out the degree of user interaction within the group and the core users in the group.
